Our Story

Honoring a Legacy of Love

The Annor Yeboah Care Foundation was established in loving memory of Late Professor Bishop Dr. Augustine Annor Yeboah, founder and Presiding Bishop of Christian Praise International Centre, who unofficially began this philanthropic work in 1994.

Hailing from a deprived tribe and family in the Brong Ahafo region, Bishop Dr. Annor-Yeboah completed his education from primary to tertiary level through the benevolence of friends, family, and well-wishers. Despite the challenges, he rose to become a Professor in Christian Education and a renowned philanthropist, supporting young adults through tertiary education and vocational training until his passing in 2014.

Before the foundation's official registration, Bishop Dr. Annor-Yeboah personally supported over 450 students in formal tertiary institutions and informal sectors, taking them to universities, IT schools, beauty industry, driving schools, and music schools. Today, his widowed wife and five daughters, assisted by renowned trustees, continue his philanthropic legacy.

Since official registration, the foundation in collaboration with Jayana Help The Needy Foundation has graduated over 245 students in the beauty industry and 178 students in formal education from preschool to primary level. Between 2019 to date, we have invested GHC 162,000 in partial fee payments for students across various educational levels.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To continue the inspired legacy of the Late Professor Bishop Dr. Augustine Annor Yeboah by creating a Ghana where every individual has access to quality education, vocational training, and the support needed to reach their full potential.

Our Mission

To continue improving the quality of life of people by investing in them through the legacy of Bishop Augustine Annor-Yeboah. We achieve this through: iCare Educational Fund (subsidizing fees for students in formal education), Annor Yeboah Vocational Fund (supporting apprentices and artisans), and The Donations Hub (distributing clothing, shoes, bags, and toys to communities across Ghana).
